U.S. Wireless Telecom Churn Prediction & Retention Strategy

End-to-end churn prediction for a major U.S. wireless telecom: data cleaning, descriptive insights, supervised modeling, and actionable retention strategy

Why This Project?

Problem Statement

Customer churn directly impacts revenue and growth in the telecommunications industry. The company faced increasing churn rates and needed a data-driven approach to identify at-risk customers and improve retention strategies.

This project addresses the client’s critical business need by:

  • (1) developing a statistical model to accurately forecast churn probability
  • (2) establishing target customer profiles to identify segments and tailor effective retention strategies.

The deliverable covers data manipulation and cleaning, descriptive analysis, supervised modeling and comparison, result interpretation, prioritized findings, recommendations.

Objectives & Goals

  • Develop a statistical model to accurately forecast churn probability
  • Establish target customer profiles to identify segments and tailor effective retention strategies

Data Description

Telecom Customer Dataset

  • Customer-level features include demographics (income group, marital status, occupation)
  • Usage metrics (monthly minutes, outbound calls, number of handsets)
  • Service quality indicators (dropped calls, handset issues, number of calls to retention team)
  • Interaction history (retention calls, offers accepted, referrals)
  • Target variable: churn (Yes/No)

How I Built This

Development Approach

  • Audit and clean data
  • Perform descriptive analysis to understand demographics, behaviors, and interactions
  • Train multiple supervised models
  • Evaluate using confusion matrices and ROC-optimized cutoff
  • Generate a ranked list of customers most likely to churn for targeted retention

Visualizations & Insights

Model Performance (Cutoff = 0.5)

Naïve Bayes delivers the strongest overall performance among tested models with Accuracy ≈ 74.8% and AUC ≈ 0.834. Random Forest and Bagging perform similarly, while KNN underperforms. A ROC-optimized cutoff (0.426) further improves sensitivity to churners while maintaining reasonable specificity.

Customer Behaviors

Monthly minutes are positively skewed; a small segment are heavy users (e.g., business users) suggesting tailored plans.

Service Quality Issues

Dropped calls appear across clusters and likely drive dissatisfaction; handset issues affect most customers at least once.

Customer Interactions

Low levels of retention calls, offers accepted, referrals, and retention-team calls indicate weak engagement.

Targeting Efficiency

Gains analysis: focusing top 20–30% captures ≈35.5–51% of churners (lift ≈168–155%).

Actionable Business Recommendations

Strategic Implementation

Translate predictive model results, hierarchical clustering, and descriptive analyses into retention playbooks tailored by segment:

  • Cluster 1: High-value, high-usage, many dropped calls
    Priority care and network quality fixes; personalized offers; proactive outreach.
  • Cluster 2: Moderate usage, elevated dropped calls
    Monitor network quality; mid-range plans aligned to usage; targeted service credits.
  • Cluster 3: Large, low-frequency users
    Survey needs; promote cost-effective low-usage packages; value messaging.
  • Cluster 4: Low engagement, low revenue
    Research low engagement drivers; align offerings; consider win-back vs. deprioritization.
  • Cross-cutting improvements
    Reduce dropped calls; address handset issues via maintenance/updates; improve interaction channels; prioritize top 3,000 churners.
