Live Tweets Analysis - NLP

Data-driven analysis of Twitter engagement patterns to optimize social media strategy and boost TV viewership during live race events

Problem Statement

Social media platforms, particularly Twitter, have become vital for real-time engagement during live sports events. The immediacy of Twitter allows fans to share opinions and interact, generating valuable data on public sentiment and interest.

However, there're some challenges in leveraging this data to:

  • Maximize Online Engagement: Identify tweet characteristics that drive high interaction (likes, retweets, comments) during live broadcasts
  • Boost TV Viewership: Understand how tweet content correlates with TV viewership and whether it can predict viewership variations
  • Optimize Marketing Strategies: Develop data-driven approaches to enhance brand visibility and fan experience during race events

Objectives & Goals

  • Identify tweet features (sentiment, topics, timing) that drive high online engagement during Supercars live broadcasts
  • Determine tweet content characteristics associated with increased TV viewership and assess their predictive power
  • Provide data-driven recommendations to enhance social media strategies and fan engagement
  • Build predictive models to forecast engagement and viewership patterns
  • Optimize tweet timing and content for maximum impact during race events

ðŸ“Š Data Description

Dataset Overview

The analysis utilized two primary datasets integrated for comprehensive insights:

  • Twitter Dataset: Tweet data from Supercars Championship events, including text content, timestamps, likes, retweets, and comments
  • TV Ratings Dataset: TV viewership data at 15-minute intervals, including viewer counts and broadcast channel details
  • Features: Tweet text, timestamps, engagement metrics, sentiment scores, topic IDs, viewer counts, and channel information
  • Preprocessing: Normalized text via tokenization, decapitalization, punctuation removal, and stop word elimination using NLTK
  • Feature Engineering: Created engagement_score, sentiment categories, topic modeling, and temporal features

Data Preparation & Quality

  • Handled missing values and removed empty columns
  • Converted timestamps to datetime format for alignment
  • Aggregated tweet metrics into 15-minute intervals to align with TV ratings
  • Created engagement_score by summing likes, retweets, and comments per interval
  • Derived sentiment_score using VADER (-1 to +1) and sentiment categories
  • Applied LDA topic modeling to categorize tweets into 5 topics
  • Final dataset: Integrated Twitter and TV ratings with 80+ features

Strategic Implementation

Based on the comprehensive EDA and predictive modeling of Twitter engagement patterns and TV viewership correlations, here are key recommendations to enhance social media strategy and audience engagement:

  • Optimize Tweet Timing
    Schedule tweets between 10:00â€“14:00 and 17:00â€“18:00 AEST, especially on Sundays, to align with peak engagement and viewership periods
  • Craft Player-Centric Content
    Focus tweets on individual drivers (e.g., Chaz Mostert) to leverage their emotional appeal, as number_of_player strongly predicts engagement (r=0.69)
  • Incorporate High-Impact Keywords
    Use hashtags like #Bathurst1000, #V8SC, and driver names in tweets, as identified by topic modeling, to boost engagement

  • Combine Features for Impact
    Create tweets integrating driver mentions, high-impact hashtags, optimal timing, and channel references for maximum engagement and viewership
  • Sustain Viewership Post-Peak
    Address mid-race viewership drop-off by posting driver interviews, behind-the-scenes content, or fan polls during lower-engagement periods (post-18:00)
